Pro-Tip

Chronic cognitive-emotional hyperarousal associated with elevated metabolic rate, sympathetic overactivation, and chronic inflammation is a common substrate of insomnia. Hyperarousal may be understood as circadian alertness (wakefulness) that has gone awry and overrides both normal sleep drive and the excessive daytime sleepiness one would expect with chronic insomnia.

If there is a secret to a good night's sleep, it is a good day's waking. Most chronic insomnia results not from insufficient sleepiness, but from excessive wakefulness. Letting go of something to sleep refers to an approach concerned with reducing the noise of this excessive wakefulness.

Trying to promote healthy sleep without considering dreams is like trying to promote healthy nutrition without regard for the taste of food. Sleep disorders, in part, are chronic skirmishes between nature and culture—between linear and cyclic time.

Because even small amounts of light can trickle across closed eyelids and suppress melatonin, sleeping in total darkness or with a sleep mask is ideal.

  • Cognitive impairment

Reducing Body Noise

  • Manage comorbid conditions such as depression and chronic pain.
  • Manage medication side effects.
  • Limit alcohol and caffeine use.
  • Address women's health issues like PMDD or menopause.

Dysfunctional Thoughts About Sleep

  • ""I should sleep at least 8 hours every night.""
  • ""I should fall asleep quickly.""
  • ""I should always sleep through the night.""
  • ""I can and must get myself to sleep.""
  • ""I should just rest in bed if I cannot sleep.""
  • ""I will have a terrible day if I do not sleep well.""

Promoting Healthy Dreaming

  • Identify and manage dream thieves.
  • Arise slowly in the morning to enhance recall.
  • Journal or talk about dreams.
  • Join a dream circle or support group.
  • Note dreamlike aspects of waking life.

  • Restoring dream health

Stimulus Control Therapy Instructions

  1. Get into bed only when sleepy.
  2. Use the bed only for sleep and sexual activity.
  3. Do not watch the clock.
  4. If awake after 15 minutes, leave the bedroom and engage in restful activity.
  5. Keep a fixed morning rising time.
  6. Avoid napping until nighttime sleep normalizes.

Common Sources of Bedroom Toxicity

  • Pesticide-laden fabrics in bedding
  • Synthetic materials in mattresses
  • Outgassing from furnishings
  • Polluted indoor air
  • Electromagnetic fields

Regulating Circadian Rhythms

  • Use phototherapy with timed light exposure.
  • Maintain a regular sleep-wake pattern.
  • Simulate dusk by dimming lights 1–2 hours before sleep.
  • Supplement with melatonin.
  • Sleep in total darkness.

Creating a Sense of Sanctuary

  • Establish the bedroom as a stress-free zone.
  • Limit exposure to stressful imagery.
  • Conceal clocks.
  • Ensure personal safety.
  • Maintain peace with your sleep partner.

Prevention Prescription

  • Recognize the value and joy of sleep.
  • Take good care of your waking life.
  • Learn and practice relaxation and stress management.
  • Regulate circadian rhythms.
  • Optimize bedroom health.
  • Be mindful of medications.
